AI Course Scheduling Tool

(Ongoing Project • PI, CHSS Research Grant 2026)

Project Context

This ongoing project explores how AI can support small academic programs with long-term course planning. As faculty in Technical Communication at Sam Houston State University, we often rely on lengthy meetings each semester to determine which courses should be offered next term. Our decisions must consider past patterns of course rotation, required sequences for undergraduate and graduate students, and the needs of our certificate and minor programs. Yet we lack an efficient way to visualize these patterns or predict future needs.

To address this challenge, I began developing an AI-assisted course scheduling system designed specifically for small programs like ours, where faculty numbers are limited and every scheduling decision has major downstream effects. Using AI tools to analyze historical course offerings, I created an initial model that identifies scheduling patterns and aligns future offerings with program requirements.

Current Progress

Building on this early progress, I transformed the work into a case study article and secured a CHSS Collaborative Research Grant.

Williams (Eds.), Preparing for (Almost) Everything: A Practical Guide to Tech Comm Budgets, Staffing, and Curriculum Development. Utah State University Press / University Press of Colorado.

Principal Investigator (PI): Smart Course Scheduling: An Interdisciplinary AI Initiative for Small Academic Programs, CHSS Collaborative Research Grant, Sam Houston State University (2026, funded: $5,000).

As one of the university’s AI Faculty Fellows, I invited the other Fellows from engineering, education, and philosophy to join this project so we can collaboratively develop this tool and expand its applicability across disciplines.

Next Stage

The next stage of this project focuses on developing an accessible web-based interface that allows departments to view historical patterns, adjust program requirements, and generate updated schedules with a single click. Our long-term goal is to make this an adaptable tool that small academic programs can immediately use, freeing faculty time, improving student outcomes, and supporting sustainable curriculum planning.
